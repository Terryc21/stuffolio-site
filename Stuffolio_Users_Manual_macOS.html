Adding Your First Product

Opening the Add Items Menu

  1. Click My Products in the sidebar
  2. Click the + button in the toolbar (top of window)
  3. The Add Items chooser appears

Alternative Methods

  • Keyboard shortcut: Press ‚åòN from anywhere in the app
  • Menu bar: Go to File > New Product

Choose Your Entry Mode

The Add Items menu offers five options based on your situation:

  • Something I just got ‚Äî Best for new purchases. Captures purchase date, price, warranty info, and receipt details. Warranty defaults to 12 months.
  • Something I already have ‚Äî For items you've owned for a while. Starts with a photo, optionally uses Stuff Scout AI to identify and value the item. Focuses on current market value rather than purchase history.
  • Multiple identical items ‚Äî Quickly add multiple units of the same product (e.g., 4 dining chairs). Enter details once, set quantity.
  • Record a donation ‚Äî Track items donated to charity for tax deduction purposes.
  • Use Stuff Scout AI ‚Äî Take a photo and let AI identify the item, estimate its value, and fill in details automatically.

Something I Just Got (New Purchases)

  1. Click Something I just got
  2. The Add Product form opens with all sections expanded
  3. Fill in the details (see "Filling Out the Product Form" below)

Something I Already Have (Existing Items)

For cataloging items you've owned for a while ‚Äî furniture, heirlooms, tools, collectibles.

  1. Click Something I already have
  2. Choose how to add a photo:
    • Choose from Library ‚Äî Select an existing photo
    • Skip photo ‚Äî Go directly to the form
  3. If you have AI access, optionally analyze the photo with Stuff Scout
  4. The form opens with your photo attached and Current Market Value field emphasized
Insurance Tip: For existing items, enter the Current Market Value ‚Äî what it would cost to replace the item today. This is what insurance companies need for claims.

Adding Photos to Products

Photos help identify products and serve as documentation for warranty claims. Stuffolio can also analyze your photos to automatically suggest categories and extract product details like brand, model, serial number, and price.

Photo Analysis Options

When you add a photo while creating or editing a product, Stuffolio can analyze it to help fill in product information:

Three Analysis Options

Option What It Does Best For
Suggest Category Uses on-device AI to identify the product type Product photos showing the item clearly
Extract Details Uses cloud OCR to read text and fill form fields Receipts, labels, nameplates with visible text
Just Add Photo Adds the photo without analysis Any photo you want to keep

Extract Details (OCR Auto-Fill)

The Extract Details option can automatically populate form fields by reading text from your photo:

What It Extracts

  • Brand/Manufacturer ‚Äî From product labels or receipts
  • Model Number ‚Äî From nameplates or packaging
  • Serial Number ‚Äî From product labels
  • Purchase Price ‚Äî From receipts
  • Purchase Date ‚Äî From receipts
  • Store/Retailer ‚Äî From receipts

How to Use

  1. Add a photo showing a receipt, label, or nameplate
  2. When the Photo Assessment appears, click Extract Details
  3. Wait for OCR processing (requires internet)
  4. Extracted fields are automatically filled in the form
  5. Review and edit as needed
Tips for Best OCR Results:
  • Photograph labels and receipts straight-on (not at an angle)
  • Ensure good lighting with no shadows on text
  • Get close enough that text is clearly readable
  • For receipts, capture the full receipt including store name and date

Photo Quality Assessment

When you add a photo, Stuffolio analyzes it on-device to assess quality:

  • Blur Detection ‚Äî Identifies if the photo is too blurry for reliable analysis
  • Text Detection ‚Äî Checks if readable text is present (important for OCR)
  • Resolution Check ‚Äî Ensures the image is high enough quality

Each analysis option shows a suitability indicator:

  • Good match ‚Äî Photo is well-suited for this analysis
  • May work ‚Äî Analysis might succeed, but results may vary
  • Poor match ‚Äî Photo unlikely to yield good results

Using the Smart Scanner

The Smart Scanner reads barcodes to automatically fill in product information.

Note: On Mac, the Smart Scanner uses Continuity Camera with your iPhone. Make sure your iPhone is nearby and signed into the same iCloud account.

Opening the Smart Scanner

  1. Click Actions in the sidebar
  2. Under Acquire, click Smart Scanner
  3. Or click the + button and select Smart Scanner

Using Continuity Camera

  1. When Smart Scanner opens, a camera view appears
  2. Your nearby iPhone's camera activates automatically
  3. Point your iPhone at the UPC barcode on the product packaging
  4. Hold steady 6-12 inches away
  5. When detected:
    • You hear a beep
    • The barcode number appears on screen
    • Product lookup begins automatically

Tips for Better Scanning

  • Ensure good lighting (avoid shadows on the barcode)
  • Make sure the barcode isn't wrinkled or damaged
  • Hold the phone parallel to the barcode (not at an angle)

Scanning Additional Barcodes (Optional)

  1. Click Scan Model/Serial
  2. Point at barcodes on the product itself (not packaging)
  3. The scanner identifies the barcode type
  4. Information fills automatically
  5. Click Done when finished, or Skip to continue

Review and Save

  1. A form appears with auto-detected information
  2. Review each field ‚Äî click to edit if incorrect
  3. Add purchase information (date, price, warranty)
  4. Click Save or press ‚åòS

Using Stuff Scout (AI Identification)

Stuff Scout uses AI to identify antiques, collectibles, and unknown items from photos.

Opening Stuff Scout

  1. Click Actions in the sidebar
  2. Under Acquire, click Stuff Scout
  3. Or click the Stuff Scout card on the Dashboard

Adding Photos

Since Mac doesn't have a built-in camera, you'll add existing photos:

Option A: Drag and Drop

  1. Open Finder and navigate to your photos
  2. Drag image files onto the Stuff Scout window
  3. Drop when you see the highlight

Option B: Choose File

  1. Click Add Photo
  2. Click Choose File
  3. Navigate to and select your image
  4. Click Open

Option C: Paste from Clipboard

  1. Copy an image (‚åòC from Photos, Finder, or web)
  2. Click Paste Image

Add Multiple Photos (Recommended)

For better identification, add photos showing:

  • Maker's Mark ‚Äî Signatures, stamps, or labels
  • Bottom/Back ‚Äî Often has identifying marks
  • Details ‚Äî Close-ups of unique features
  • Damage ‚Äî Any wear or repairs

You can add up to 5 photos.

Identification Results

Click the Identify button and wait 10-30 seconds. Results include:

  • What It Is ‚Äî Description of the item
  • Maker/Manufacturer ‚Äî Who made it
  • Era ‚Äî Approximate age or period
  • Estimated Value Range ‚Äî Low to high market value
  • Rarity ‚Äî Common to Extremely Rare

Background Processing

You don't need to keep Stuffolio in the foreground while Stuff Scout analyzes your photos:

  1. After clicking Identify, you can switch to another app or minimize the window
  2. Analysis continues in the background
  3. When complete, you'll receive a notification: "Stuff Scout Complete"
  4. Click the notification to view your results
Tip: Background processing is especially useful for antiques and collectibles that require more detailed analysis.

Refining Results

If the AI identification isn't quite right, you can provide additional context:

  1. Click Not quite right? on the results screen
  2. Fill in what you know: Product Name, Manufacturer, Model, Era
  3. Add photos of maker's marks or labels (click Add or Paste)
  4. Click Re-analyze to get updated results

Each refinement uses 1 AI query from your allowance.

Saving Results

Add to Inventory: Click to open a preview sheet with pre-filled product details.

Save to History: Bookmark results for later without adding to inventory.

Receipt Pairing (Optional)

When adding a Stuff Scout result to inventory, you can pair it with a receipt to auto-fill purchase details:

  1. Click Add to Inventory to open the preview sheet
  2. In the Purchase Receipt section, click Add Receipt Photo
  3. Select a photo of your receipt
  4. Stuffolio extracts store name, purchase date, and price automatically
  5. Review the extracted data, then click Add to Inventory to continue
Tip: Receipt Pairing combines product identification from Stuff Scout with purchase details from your receipt ‚Äî creating complete records without manual typing.

Auto-fill from Receipt

If you've already attached receipt images to a product, you can extract purchase details without re-scanning:

  1. Open the product and click Edit
  2. Look for the Auto-fill from Receipt button in the Images section
  3. Click it to have Stuffolio analyze your attached receipt images
  4. Review the extracted store name, purchase date, and price
  5. Click Save to apply the changes
Tip: Auto-fill from Receipt works with any receipt images you've already added ‚Äî no need to import new photos.

Auto-Cached Recent Scans

Stuff Scout automatically saves your last 25 scans so you never lose an identification. Every time you complete a scan, it's automatically stored ‚Äî no extra steps required.

  • Automatic saving ‚Äî Scans are saved as soon as analysis completes
  • Last 25 scans kept ‚Äî When you exceed 25, the oldest scan is automatically removed
  • No action needed ‚Äî Just scan and your results are preserved
Tip: Recent scans are perfect for quick lookups. If you want to keep a scan forever, promote it to a Bookmark.

Scout History

Access all your past Stuff Scout identifications in Scout History, organized into two tabs:

Recent Tab

Shows your auto-cached scans (last 25):

  • Click any recent scan to view details
  • Right-click (or Control-click) and choose Save as Bookmark to keep it permanently
  • Click Clear All to remove all recent scans

Bookmarks Tab

Shows scans you've saved permanently:

  • Bookmarks never expire or get automatically deleted
  • Add notes and tags to organize your collection
  • Right-click to delete individual bookmarks
Tip: Found something valuable? Right-click any recent scan to save it as a permanent bookmark before it gets pushed out by newer scans.

Managing Warranties

Adding Warranty Information

When adding a new product:

  1. In the Add Product form, scroll to Warranty section
  2. Click Warranty Expiration Date and select the date
  3. Click Warranty Type: Standard, Extended, or Lifetime
  4. Optionally add Warranty Notes

For existing products:

  1. Open the product
  2. Click Edit in the toolbar
  3. Fill in the warranty fields
  4. Click Save or press ‚åòS

Warranty Status Badges

  • Active ‚Äî Warranty currently valid
  • Warning ‚Äî Expiring within 30 days
  • Expired ‚Äî No longer covered
  • Lifetime ‚Äî Permanent coverage

Setting Warranty Reminders

  1. Go to Settings (or press ‚åò,)
  2. Click Notifications
  3. Toggle on Warranty Expiring and Coverage Phase Changes
  4. Set Advance Notice: 7, 14, or 30 days before

Tip: With Coverage Phase Changes enabled, you'll receive notifications before your warranty transitions (e.g., 30 days before Full ‚Üí Parts Only).

Coverage Types

Different warranties offer different levels of coverage:

To set coverage type: Expand Coverage Details in the warranty section and select the appropriate type.

Coverage Phases (Tiered Warranties)

Many warranties change coverage over time. For example, a 5-year appliance warranty might offer:

  • Year 1: Full coverage (parts + labor)
  • Years 2-3: Parts only
  • Years 4-5: Limited coverage

To add coverage phases:

  1. In the warranty section, expand Coverage Details
  2. Click Add Coverage Phase
  3. Select the coverage type for this phase
  4. Set the start and end dates
  5. Optionally add a deductible amount
  6. Press ‚åòS to save
  7. Repeat for additional phases

The detail view shows your current coverage phase and when the next change occurs.

Tracking Deductibles

Record out-of-pocket costs for warranty claims:

  1. Expand Coverage Details in the warranty section
  2. Enter the Deductible amount
  3. For tiered warranties, you can set different deductibles per phase

AppleCare+ Tracking

Stuffolio makes it easy to track AppleCare+ coverage for your Apple products.

How Sync Works

  • Conflict resolution ‚Äî Most recent change wins

Sync Status

In Settings > Data, look for:

  • ‚ö†Ô∏è Offline ‚Äî No internet connection

Offline Mode & Auto-Sync

New in Sprint 5: Stuffolio now works seamlessly even when you're offline.

How Offline Mode Works

  • Make changes offline ‚Äî Add, edit, or delete products without an internet connection
  • Automatic queuing ‚Äî All changes are saved locally and queued for sync
  • Auto-sync when online ‚Äî When your Mac reconnects to the internet, queued changes sync automatically
  • No manual action needed ‚Äî You don't need to press any buttons or trigger sync manually

What You Can Do Offline

  • Add new products
  • Edit existing products
  • Add photos (they'll upload when you're back online)
  • Delete products
  • Browse your inventory
  • Search and filter products

Viewing Pending Changes

While offline, you can see how many changes are waiting to sync:

  1. Go to Settings > Data
  2. Look for the sync status indicator
  3. If offline, it shows: "Offline ‚Äî X changes pending"
  4. When you reconnect, these changes upload automatically
Tip: Offline mode is perfect for inventory work in areas with poor connectivity, like garages, basements, or storage units. Just keep working ‚Äî everything syncs when you're back online.

Conflict Resolution

If you edit the same product on multiple devices while offline:

  • The most recent change wins
  • Stuffolio uses timestamps to determine which version to keep
  • No data is lost ‚Äî earlier versions are preserved in sync history

Troubleshooting

Products Not Syncing Between Devices

  1. Check iCloud is enabled in Stuffolio: Go to Settings > Data, verify iCloud Sync is on
  2. Verify iCloud in System Settings: Open System Settings > [Your Apple ID] > iCloud, ensure Stuffolio is enabled
  3. Check iCloud storage: Ensure you have available storage
  4. Try resetting sync: Toggle iCloud Sync off, wait 10 seconds, toggle back on

Smart Scanner Not Working

  1. Ensure your iPhone is nearby and unlocked
  2. Both devices must be signed into the same iCloud account
  3. Ensure Bluetooth and Wi-Fi are enabled on both devices
  4. Check Continuity Camera settings:
    • Mac: System Settings > General > AirDrop & Handoff
    • iPhone: Settings > General > AirPlay & Handoff

AI Features Not Responding

  1. Check internet connection
  2. Wait 30 seconds and retry
  3. If rate limited, wait a few minutes
  4. Quit and reopen the app

App Running Slowly

  1. Quit and reopen Stuffolio
  2. Check available disk space (Apple menu > About This Mac > Storage)
  3. Restart your Mac
  4. Check for updates in the App Store

Notifications Not Appearing

  1. Open System Settings
  2. Click Notifications
  3. Find Stuffolio and ensure notifications are allowed
  4. Verify Focus mode isn't blocking notifications
