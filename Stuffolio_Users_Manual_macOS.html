Adding Your First Product

Using the Toolbar

  1. Click My Products in the sidebar
  2. Click the + button in the toolbar (top of window)
  3. A menu appears with options:
    • Add Product ‚Äî Manual entry
    • Smart Scanner ‚Äî Scan barcode (requires iPhone with Continuity Camera)
    • Stuff Scout ‚Äî AI identification
  4. Click Add Product
  5. The Add Product form opens in a sheet

Using the Keyboard Shortcut

  1. Press ‚åòN from anywhere in the app
  2. The Add Product form opens directly

Using the Menu Bar

  1. Go to File > New Product (or press ‚åòN)
  2. The Add Product form opens

Filling Out the Product Form

The form has these fields (only Product Name is required):

Step 1: Enter Basic Information

  1. Product Name (Required) ‚Äî Click the text field, type the product name (e.g., "Samsung 65-inch TV")
  2. Manufacturer ‚Äî Click the text field, type the brand name (e.g., "Samsung")
  3. Model Number ‚Äî Enter the model number from the product label (e.g., "QN65Q80CAFXZA")
  4. Serial Number ‚Äî Enter the serial number (important for warranty claims)

Step 2: Choose a Category

  1. Click the Category dropdown menu
  2. A list appears with 20+ categories: Electronics, Appliances, Furniture, Tools, Automotive, Sports, etc.
  3. Click to select the appropriate category

Step 3: Set Location

  1. Click the Location field
  2. Type where the product is stored (e.g., "Living Room", "Garage")

Step 4: Enter Purchase Information

  1. Purchase Date ‚Äî Click the date field, use the picker or type directly (e.g., "1/15/2024")
  2. Purchase Price ‚Äî Enter the amount you paid (numbers only)
  3. Retailer (Optional) ‚Äî Enter where you bought it

Step 5: Enter Warranty Information

  1. Warranty Expiration Date ‚Äî Select the date your warranty ends
  2. Warranty Type ‚Äî Click to select: Standard, Extended, or Lifetime
  3. Warranty Notes (Optional) ‚Äî Add any coverage details

Step 6: Add Notes

  1. Click the Notes field
  2. Type any additional information (e.g., "Gift from Mom", "Bought on sale")

Step 7: Save the Product

  1. Review all entered information
  2. Click the Save button in the toolbar
  3. Or press ‚åòS to save
  4. The product is added to your inventory

Adding Photos to Products

Photos help identify products and serve as documentation for warranty claims.

Method 1: Paste from Clipboard (Most Common on Mac)

Important: You must copy an image BEFORE clicking the Paste button. If nothing happens when you click Paste Image, no image is currently on your clipboard ‚Äî go back and copy an image first.

Step 1: Copy an image to your clipboard

From the Photos app:

  1. Open Photos
  2. Find and select the image you want
  3. Press ‚åòC to copy

From Finder:

  1. Navigate to the image file
  2. Click to select it
  3. Press ‚åòC to copy

From a web browser:

  1. Right-click on any image
  2. Select Copy Image from the menu

Step 2: Paste into Stuffolio

  1. Return to Stuffolio's Add/Edit Product window
  2. In the Photos section, click the Paste Image button
  3. The image appears in the Photos grid

Method 2: Drag and Drop

  1. Open Finder or Photos app in a separate window
  2. Arrange windows so you can see both Stuffolio and the source
  3. Find the image you want to add
  4. Click and hold the image file
  5. Drag it over to Stuffolio's Photos section
  6. When you see the drop zone highlight, release
Tip: You can drag multiple images at once by selecting several files in Finder (hold ‚åò while clicking), then dragging them all together.

Method 3: Choose File

  1. In the Photos section, click the + button or Add Photo
  2. Click Choose File from the menu
  3. A file browser window opens
  4. Navigate to your image file (Recent, Documents, Downloads, or iCloud Drive)
  5. Click the image file to select it
  6. Click Open

Managing Existing Photos

To view a photo full-size: Click any photo thumbnail. Scroll to zoom in/out. Click outside or press Escape to close.

To delete a photo: Hover over the photo, click the X button. Or select the photo and press Delete.

To reorder photos: Click and hold a photo thumbnail, drag to the new position. The first photo becomes the main product image.

Photo Types to Add

  • Product Photo ‚Äî Main image showing the product
  • Receipt ‚Äî Proof of purchase for warranty claims
  • Label/Nameplate ‚Äî Shows model and serial numbers
  • Warranty Card ‚Äî Coverage documentation
  • Packaging ‚Äî UPC barcode and specifications

Using the Smart Scanner

The Smart Scanner reads barcodes to automatically fill in product information.

Note: On Mac, the Smart Scanner uses Continuity Camera with your iPhone. Make sure your iPhone is nearby and signed into the same iCloud account.

Opening the Smart Scanner

  1. Click Actions in the sidebar
  2. Under Acquire, click Smart Scanner
  3. Or click the + button and select Smart Scanner

Using Continuity Camera

  1. When Smart Scanner opens, a camera view appears
  2. Your nearby iPhone's camera activates automatically
  3. Point your iPhone at the UPC barcode on the product packaging
  4. Hold steady 6-12 inches away
  5. When detected:
    • You hear a beep
    • The barcode number appears on screen
    • Product lookup begins automatically

Tips for Better Scanning

  • Ensure good lighting (avoid shadows on the barcode)
  • Make sure the barcode isn't wrinkled or damaged
  • Hold the phone parallel to the barcode (not at an angle)

Scanning Additional Barcodes (Optional)

  1. Click Scan Model/Serial
  2. Point at barcodes on the product itself (not packaging)
  3. The scanner identifies the barcode type
  4. Information fills automatically
  5. Click Done when finished, or Skip to continue

Review and Save

  1. A form appears with auto-detected information
  2. Review each field ‚Äî click to edit if incorrect
  3. Add purchase information (date, price, warranty)
  4. Click Save or press ‚åòS

Using Stuff Scout (AI Identification)

Stuff Scout uses AI to identify antiques, collectibles, and unknown items from photos.

Opening Stuff Scout

  1. Click Actions in the sidebar
  2. Under Acquire, click Stuff Scout
  3. Or click the Stuff Scout card on the Dashboard

Adding Photos

Since Mac doesn't have a built-in camera, you'll add existing photos:

Option A: Drag and Drop

  1. Open Finder and navigate to your photos
  2. Drag image files onto the Stuff Scout window
  3. Drop when you see the highlight

Option B: Choose File

  1. Click Add Photo
  2. Click Choose File
  3. Navigate to and select your image
  4. Click Open

Option C: Paste from Clipboard

  1. Copy an image (‚åòC from Photos, Finder, or web)
  2. Click Paste Image

Add Multiple Photos (Recommended)

For better identification, add photos showing:

  • Maker's Mark ‚Äî Signatures, stamps, or labels
  • Bottom/Back ‚Äî Often has identifying marks
  • Details ‚Äî Close-ups of unique features
  • Damage ‚Äî Any wear or repairs

You can add up to 5 photos.

Identification Results

Click the Identify button and wait 10-30 seconds. Results include:

  • What It Is ‚Äî Description of the item
  • Maker/Manufacturer ‚Äî Who made it
  • Era ‚Äî Approximate age or period
  • Estimated Value Range ‚Äî Low to high market value
  • Rarity ‚Äî Common to Extremely Rare

Refining Results

If the AI identification isn't quite right, you can provide additional context:

  1. Click Not quite right? on the results screen
  2. Fill in what you know: Product Name, Manufacturer, Model, Era
  3. Add photos of maker's marks or labels (click Add or Paste)
  4. Click Re-analyze to get updated results

Each refinement uses 1 AI query from your allowance.

Saving Results

Add to Inventory: Click to create a product with pre-filled information.

Save to History: Bookmark results for later without adding to inventory.

Managing Warranties

Adding Warranty Information

When adding a new product:

  1. In the Add Product form, scroll to Warranty section
  2. Click Warranty Expiration Date and select the date
  3. Click Warranty Type: Standard, Extended, or Lifetime
  4. Optionally add Warranty Notes

For existing products:

  1. Open the product
  2. Click Edit in the toolbar
  3. Fill in the warranty fields
  4. Click Save or press ‚åòS

Warranty Status Badges

  • Active ‚Äî Warranty currently valid
  • Warning ‚Äî Expiring within 30 days
  • Expired ‚Äî No longer covered
  • Lifetime ‚Äî Permanent coverage

Setting Warranty Reminders

  1. Go to Settings (or press ‚åò,)
  2. Click Notifications
  3. Toggle on Warranty Expiring and Coverage Phase Changes
  4. Set Advance Notice: 7, 14, or 30 days before

Tip: With Coverage Phase Changes enabled, you'll receive notifications before your warranty transitions (e.g., 30 days before Full ‚Üí Parts Only).

Coverage Types

Different warranties offer different levels of coverage:

TypeWhat's CoveredExample
Full CoverageParts + LaborFactory warranty on new appliance
Parts OnlyReplacement parts (you pay labor)Extended coverage years 2-3
Labor OnlyLabor costs (you pay for parts)Service contracts
LimitedSpecific components onlyPowertrain warranty on vehicles

To set coverage type: Expand Coverage Details in the warranty section and select the appropriate type.

Coverage Phases (Tiered Warranties)

Many warranties change coverage over time. For example, a 5-year appliance warranty might offer:

  • Year 1: Full coverage (parts + labor)
  • Years 2-3: Parts only
  • Years 4-5: Limited coverage

To add coverage phases:

  1. In the warranty section, expand Coverage Details
  2. Click Add Coverage Phase
  3. Select the coverage type for this phase
  4. Set the start and end dates
  5. Optionally add a deductible amount
  6. Press ‚åòS to save
  7. Repeat for additional phases

The detail view shows your current coverage phase and when the next change occurs.

Tracking Deductibles

Record out-of-pocket costs for warranty claims:

  1. Expand Coverage Details in the warranty section
  2. Enter the Deductible amount
  3. For tiered warranties, you can set different deductibles per phase

Reviewing Data Quality

Keep your inventory complete and organized with the Data Review feature.

Accessing Data Review

  1. Go to Settings in the sidebar (or press ‚åò,)
  2. Click Data Management
  3. Scroll to the Data Quality section
  4. Click Review Items

What Data Review Shows

Items Without Photos:

  • Products that don't have any photos attached
  • Click an item to open it and add photos
  • Items are sorted by most recent first

Potential Duplicates:

  • Products with the same title and manufacturer
  • Grouped together for easy comparison
  • Click any item to view details and verify

Using Data Review

To add photos to an item:

  1. Click the item row (anywhere except the OK button)
  2. The item detail view opens
  3. Click Add Image to attach photos
  4. Navigate back to continue reviewing

To dismiss an item from future reviews:

  1. Click the OK button next to the item
  2. The item is marked as reviewed
  3. It won't appear in future Data Reviews

To reset dismissed items:

  1. Scroll to the bottom of Data Review
  2. Find "X items reviewed and hidden"
  3. Click Show Again
  4. Confirm to reset all dismissed items

Tips

  • Review your data periodically to keep it complete
  • Adding photos helps with insurance claims and identification
  • Dismissing an item doesn't delete it ‚Äî just hides it from the review list

Accessibility

Stuffolio is designed to be accessible to everyone.

VoiceOver

Full VoiceOver support throughout the app. To enable: Press ‚åòF5 or go to System Settings > Accessibility > VoiceOver.

Keyboard Navigation

  1. Go to System Settings > Accessibility > Keyboard
  2. Enable Full Keyboard Access
  3. Use Tab to move between controls
  4. Use Space to activate buttons

Dynamic Type

Text scales with your system settings. Go to System Settings > Accessibility > Display and adjust Text size.

Color Accessibility

  • No reliance on color alone ‚Äî status uses color + icon + text
  • Blue/Yellow/Red palette (avoids green/orange confusion for colorblind users)
  • High contrast mode supported
  • WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliant

Reduce Motion

Go to System Settings > Accessibility > Display and toggle Reduce motion for simplified animations.

Troubleshooting

Products Not Syncing Between Devices

  1. Check iCloud is enabled in Stuffolio: Go to Settings > Data, verify iCloud Sync is on
  2. Verify iCloud in System Settings: Open System Settings > [Your Apple ID] > iCloud, ensure Stuffolio is enabled
  3. Check iCloud storage: Ensure you have available storage
  4. Try resetting sync: Toggle iCloud Sync off, wait 10 seconds, toggle back on

Smart Scanner Not Working

  1. Ensure your iPhone is nearby and unlocked
  2. Both devices must be signed into the same iCloud account
  3. Ensure Bluetooth and Wi-Fi are enabled on both devices
  4. Check Continuity Camera settings:
    • Mac: System Settings > General > AirDrop & Handoff
    • iPhone: Settings > General > AirPlay & Handoff

AI Features Not Responding

  1. Check internet connection
  2. Wait 30 seconds and retry
  3. If rate limited, wait a few minutes
  4. Quit and reopen the app

App Running Slowly

  1. Quit and reopen Stuffolio
  2. Check available disk space (Apple menu > About This Mac > Storage)
  3. Restart your Mac
  4. Check for updates in the App Store

Notifications Not Appearing

  1. Open System Settings
  2. Click Notifications
  3. Find Stuffolio and ensure notifications are allowed
  4. Verify Focus mode isn't blocking notifications
