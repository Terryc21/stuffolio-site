What ‚ÄúLifecycle‚Äù Looks Like in Practice

A lifecycle approach only matters if it helps on real days: the day something breaks, the day you‚Äôre on hold with support, and the day you‚Äôre deciding whether to buy that brand again.

Warranty coverage phases (what‚Äôs covered right now)

Many warranties change over time. Instead of only storing an expiration date, Stuffolio tracks phases (for example: full coverage early on, then parts-only later). The point is simple: when something fails, you can see what you‚Äôre actually covered for today.

One-tap access at the repair shop

Stuffolio can generate Apple Wallet warranty cards so your coverage is easier to pull up quickly. (If another home inventory app offers this, we‚Äôd be happy to add it here.)

Claims and support history you won‚Äôt regret not writing down

Stuffolio keeps the context of support calls and claims ‚Äî case numbers, representative names, what was promised, shipping/RMA details, and outcomes. That history is boring until the third call, when it suddenly becomes the whole point.

Price Watch: Know replacement costs, not just what you paid

Insurance companies want to know replacement cost, not what you paid years ago. Price Watch tracks current market prices over time, shows you price history charts, and can alert you when prices drop. Prices come from multiple sources: manual entry, Stuff Scout, barcode scanning, or AI-assisted lookup.

Product memory: "Would I buy this again?"

Most of us can remember what we own, but not how it performed five or eight years later. Stuffolio helps you build a private record of what held up, what didn't, and what you'd do differently next time.

Stuffolio syncs through your personal iCloud account ‚Äî we don‚Äôt see your inventory. For optional AI features, we send only what‚Äôs needed for that specific query, and we don‚Äôt use your data to train models.
