Preparing for an Estate Sale

For families managing a parent's estate, couples downsizing, or anyone sorting through a lifetime of belongings

When it's time to sort through a lifetime of belongings, the last thing you need is more confusion. What's this worth? Who wants it? Should we sell it or donate it? Most families face these questions with no system at all -- just boxes, guesses, and stress.

Estate auction professionals use tools like WorthPoint ($30/mo) and Auction Flex (enterprise pricing) to manage this. Families use ... a spreadsheet, if they're lucky. Stuffolio fills that gap -- consumer-friendly tools for the hardest inventory job most people will ever face.

"What IS all this stuff?"

Stuff Scout -- Point your camera at any item. AI identifies it, researches its history, and estimates its value in 30 seconds.

"What's it actually worth?"

Stuff Scout + Price Watch -- Get current market values, not what was paid 20 years ago. Track replacement costs for insurance claims.

"Who wants what?"

Legacy Wishes -- Record who gets what before anyone has to guess. Family members can indicate interest or decline -- no awkward conversations needed.

"Keep it, sell it, or donate it?"

Repair, Keep, or Replace -- See repair cost vs. replacement cost, your rating, and whether it's worth keeping. Make decisions with data, not guilt.

"We need records for taxes."

Donation Tracking -- Record fair market value, recipient organization, and date for every donated item. Export for your tax preparer.

"The estate sale company needs an inventory."

Export -- Hand the estate sale company a complete inventory with photos, descriptions, and values. CSV, PDF, or Excel -- their choice.

Protecting What You Own

For homeowners, renters, and anyone who'd struggle to list their belongings from memory

After a fire, flood, or theft, insurance companies ask you to list everything you lost -- with photos, receipts, and replacement values. Most people can't. The average American household has over $100,000 in personal property, and almost half have no inventory at all.

"I need proof of what I owned."

Photos + Receipts -- Every item documented with photos, purchase price, serial numbers, and attached receipt images. All in one place.

"Insurance wants current replacement cost."

Price Watch -- That mixer you paid $350 for costs $550 to replace today. Price Watch tracks how rising prices affect what you own.

"Is this still under warranty?"

Warranty Intelligence -- Track coverage phases, not just expiration dates. Know what's covered right now -- full coverage, parts only, or expired.

"I need a report for my agent."

Insurance Export -- Generate a claim-ready report with photos, values, and documentation. CSV, PDF, or Excel.

Document everything now. Hope you never need it.

Collecting with Confidence

For thrift shoppers, antique hunters, hobbyists, and anyone who finds things and wonders "what is this?"

You're at Goodwill. There's a set of dishes that look familiar. Are they worth $5 or $500? You could Google it, scroll through forums, compare eBay listings -- or you could point your camera and know in 30 seconds.

"What is this thing?"

Stuff Scout -- Identifies items from photos. Antique vases, vintage tools, mystery kitchen gadgets -- if it exists, Scout can probably tell you what it is.

"Is it worth what they're asking?"

Stuff Scout Valuation -- Current market value based on recent sales, condition, and rarity. Make buying decisions with data.

"I want to track my collection's value over time."

Price Watch + Value Snapshots -- See how your collection's total value changes. Know when something appreciates -- or when it's time to sell.

"Which brands and products have I liked?"

Product Memory -- Rate every item 1-5 stars. Track "Would Buy Again." Filter by your ratings next time you shop. Your experience becomes your buying guide.

Planning Ahead for Family

For parents, grandparents, and anyone who wants their family to have clarity -- not confusion

When someone passes, families face difficult decisions during an already stressful time. Who gets the piano? Does anyone want the china? What about the toolbox grandpa used every weekend? Most families guess -- or argue. A few minutes of planning now saves weeks of stress later.

"We assumed the kids would want everything."

Legacy Wishes -- Document who gets what, privately and on your terms. Family members can indicate interest or decline before anyone has to guess.

"What if nobody wants it?"

Fallback Planning -- Set contingency recipients and fallback actions (donate, sell, let family decide). Every item has a plan.

"We want the whole family on the same page."

Family Sharing + CloudKit Sync -- Share wishes with family in real time. Everyone sees the same information across all their devices.

"We need something we can print and keep with important papers."

PDF Export -- Generate a formatted Legacy Wishes document. Print it, email it, or store it with your will.
